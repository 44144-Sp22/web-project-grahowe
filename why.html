Why do amateur radio?

Amateur radio, or ham radio, is a century old science experiment. It is made up of many individuals who come from all walks of life. Whether you are a college student, a doctor, black, or white, you too can become an amateur radio operator! All you need to do is take a test that is given by your regulatory agency (The Federal Communications Commission, or the FCC in the United States).

What can you do?

As an amateur radio operator, the sky is the limit! With a license, you can:

  • Talk to someone halfway across the globe
  • Talk to an astronaut on the International Space Station
  • Participate in storm chasing through programs like SkyWarn
  • Start a local community club dedicated to amateur radio
  • Get your friends and family involved
  • And so much more!

So what are you waiting for? Go ahead and give it a try!
